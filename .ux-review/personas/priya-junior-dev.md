# Persona: Priya Sharma

## Demographics
- **Role/Title:** Junior Developer, 6 months into first job
- **Age Range:** 22-26
- **Tech Comfort Level:** Intermediate (strong at coding, new to project management tools)
- **Industry/Domain:** Web Application Development

## Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal:** Pick up specs assigned to her and understand exactly what to build
- **Secondary Goals:** Learn the IDD methodology, contribute specs for small features
- **Underlying Motivation:** Wants to prove herself on the team and deliver quality work without constant hand-holding

## Context & Environment
- **Typical Workflow:** Checks the board each morning for In Progress specs, reads spec details, implements, moves to Review
- **Devices Used:** 15" laptop (1366×768), no external monitor
- **Time Constraints:** Full work day, but context-switches between coding and spec reading
- **Frequency of Use:** Daily, mainly reading specs and updating phases

## Pain Points & Frustrations
- **Current Frustrations:** Overwhelmed by too many fields; unsure which fields are required vs. optional
- **Accessibility Needs:** Prefers high contrast text; sometimes works in bright environments
- **Common Mistakes:** Doesn't understand the hierarchy (Products → Intentions → Expectations → Specs), gets lost navigating between entities

## Success Criteria
- **Task Completion:** Can find her assigned spec, understand what to build, and move it through phases
- **Time Expectation:** Under 2 minutes to find and read a specific spec
- **Satisfaction Indicators:** "I didn't need to ask anyone how to use this"

## Key Questions This Persona Would Ask
1. "Where do I find the specs assigned to me?"
2. "What's the difference between an Intention and an Expectation?"
3. "Why can't I move this spec to Ready? What's missing?"
4. "How do I get back to the product from a spec page?"
